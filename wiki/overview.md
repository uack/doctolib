# Doctolib — Executive Summary

## Company at a Glance

- **Founded**: 2013, Paris
- **Founders**: Stanislas Niox-Chateau (CEO), Ivan Schneider, Jessy Bernal
- **HQ**: Levallois-Perret (Paris suburb), France
- **Valuation**: €5.8B (Oct 2021 Series E, €500M raised — last public round)
- **Employees**: ~3,000 (2024 est.)
- **Markets**: France (dominant), Germany, Italy — UK entry via Medicus AI acquisition (May 2026)
- **Business model**: SaaS subscription for healthcare professionals; free for patients

## Scale

- **Patients**: 70M+ registered accounts
- **Healthcare professionals**: 350,000+ practitioners on the platform
- **Appointments**: 100M+ booked per year (est.)
- **Specialties**: GP, dentist, ophthalmologist, gynaecologist, physio, nurse, 50+ more

## Core Products

### Patient-side
- **Booking app**: Search and book by specialty, location, availability
- **Patient portal**: Medical history, upcoming appointments, reminders
- **Teleconsultation**: Video consultations (launched COVID-19, 2020)
- **Vaccination campaigns**: Managed French government COVID vaccine scheduling (2021) — proved scale

### Professional (Pro) side
- **Agenda management**: Digital calendar, replaces phone booking
- **Patient records (DMI)**: EHR linked to bookings
- **AI Consultation Assistant** (Oct 2024): Live transcription + structured note generation during consultations
- **AI Telephone Assistant** (Dec 2025): AI handles inbound patient calls, books autonomously

## AI Strategy

Doctolib's strategic shift: from **scheduling infrastructure** → **AI-native clinical workflow partner**.

**Timeline:**
- Oct 2024 — Consultation Assistant: AI scribe reduces doctor admin burden
- May 2025 — AI acceleration announcement: new solutions across the platform
- Dec 2025 — AI Telephone Assistant: autonomous call handling
- May 2026 — UK expansion via Medicus AI acquisition: international AI ambition

**Design principles (inferred from public statements):**
- AI augments doctors, never replaces — physician stays in control
- Privacy-first: GDPR, EU data storage, no patient data used for training without consent
- Trust is a product feature: both patients and practitioners must trust the system

## Competitive Moat

- Network effects: more patients → more practitioners → better availability
- Government partnerships (COVID vaccines) built institutional trust
- Data flywheel: 100M+ bookings/year creates unique scheduling + clinical workflow dataset
