# Competitive Landscape

## Doctolib's Core Moat

**Integrated workflow** is the differentiator. Standalone AI scribes require copy-paste from app to EHR to billing. Doctolib has all of these in one platform. This creates:
- High switching costs (practitioners would lose their entire workflow, not just one tool)
- Data network effects (100M+ bookings/year as scheduling + clinical dataset)
- Billing lock-in (financial module captures full value chain of each encounter)

## Competitor Map

### Booking / Platform

| Competitor | Markets | Position |
|---|---|---|
| DocPlanner (ZnanyLekarz, MioDottore) | Poland, Italy, Spain, Global | Aggregator model, not deep OS. $141M VC backed. Strong in fragmented Southern/Eastern Europe. |
| Cegedim / CGM (CompuGroup Medical) | France, Germany | Legacy practice management. Slow innovation cycles, legacy codebases. Vulnerable. |
| EMIS Health | UK (~57% NHS GP share) | Legacy duopoly. UnitedHealth/Optum acquisition. Criticized for dated UI, "click fatigue." |
| TPP SystmOne | UK (~42% NHS GP share) | Other half of legacy NHS duopoly. Deeply embedded but stagnant. |

### AI Scribe Point Solutions (most directly competitive with Doctolib's AI suite)

| Competitor | Key differentiator | Doctolib's gap vs. them |
|---|---|---|
| Nabla Copilot (French) | Excellent conversational UX, well-reviewed by doctors | Standalone — no native EHR/billing integration. Needs copy-paste into existing system. Doctolib is integrated. |
| Corti | Emergency/specialist triage, voice analysis | Niche specialty focus. Not general primary care. |
| Abridge / Heidi Health / Tandem | Global/regional ambient scribe | No European regulatory compliance story at Doctolib's depth |

## Why Nabla Is the Most Relevant Benchmark

Nabla is the direct design-quality benchmark for the AI Consultation and Pre-Consultation features. Their UX is considered best-in-class for the scribe experience — frictionless, conversational, minimal.

> [!key-insight] The design challenge is: match or beat Nabla's UX frictionlessness, but with Doctolib's compliance, integration, and trust story layered on top.

## UK Market: Medicus Acquisition (May 2026)

- NHS primary care = 25-year duopoly (EMIS + TPP)
- Medicus Health = first new NHS-validated GP software in 25 years (June 2025)
- Doctolib paid £100M to buy regulatory clearance + NHS integration + clinical safety certification
- Immediate plan: deploy AI Consultation + Telephone Assistants to UK GPs
- Data stays in UK (NHS IG + UK GDPR compliance)

The UK play proves Doctolib's thesis: regulatory moat is as important as product moat in healthcare.

## Interview Use

When asked "who are Doctolib's competitors?":
- Lead with the integration moat, not a competitor list
- Acknowledge Nabla as best-in-class UX in the AI scribe category
- Use the UK story to show Doctolib's regulatory differentiation strategy
