## Key Claims (indexed for wiki extraction)

### Financials
- Series G Jan 2022: €500M raised, €5.8B valuation, ~19.3x–23x ARR multiple on ~€270M ARR
- Secondary sale Q1 2026: implied €3.6B valuation (–40%), but ARR grew to ~€422M (+56%)
- 2024 ARR: €348M. 2025 ARR: ~€422M
- CEO confirmed operational profitability on BFM TV
- R&D spend: €115M–€150M annually

### Headcount
- Levallois-Perret (Paris) HQ + Berlin + Italy (post-Dottori.it acquisition)
- 2787–3100 range (some sources up to 3571) — flagged as uncertain
- UK: committed 150 hires in London R&D center (post-Medicus acquisition, May 2026)

### Product / Pricing
- Base subscription: €139/month per practitioner
- Consultation Assistant: €79/month
- AI Telephone Assistant: €99/month (GP France), €49 (paramedical)
- Full suite can reach €300–€500/practitioner/month

### AI Features
- Consultation Assistant (Oct 2024): ambient AI scribe, 1M+ consultations in first months, 50% documentation time reduction, audio deleted within 48h
- Spring 2025 acceleration: Medical Dictation, Automatic Referral Letter Generation
- Telephone Assistant (Dec 2025): built on Aaron.ai acquisition (May 2024), handles 80% of routine calls, 200K+ calls by spring 2026, saves 6h/week secretarial time
- Joint lab with Inria (€20M): "general medical intelligence system", sovereign AI goal

### UK Expansion
- Acquired Medicus Health (May 2026): £100M initial commitment
- Medicus: first new NHS GP software in 25 years to get full NHS validation (June 2025), cloud-native
- UK market duopoly: EMIS Health (~57%) + TPP SystmOne (~42%), both legacy/criticized for UI
- Medicus CEO: Emile Axelrad, retained post-acquisition
- UK data stays in UK (NHS IG + UK GDPR)

### Competition
- DocPlanner: market aggregator, $141M VC, Southern/Eastern Europe
- Nabla Copilot: French AI scribe, good UX but standalone (no native EHR/billing integration)
- Corti: emergency/specialist AI
- EMIS + TPP: UK legacy incumbents
- Key Doctolib moat: integrated workflow (calendar + EHR + billing + AI in one) vs point solutions

### Regulatory
- GDPR Article 9: health data = special category, explicit consent required
- EU AI Act: medical AI = High-Risk. Mandates human oversight (Art.14), transparency (Art.13), audit logs (Art.12)
- MDR applies simultaneously with EU AI Act
- Automation Bias risk: designers must build productive friction to prevent uncritical AI acceptance
- French HDS (Health Data Hosting) certification required

### Design System / Role
- 50+ person international design team
- Senior Staff Designer reports to VP/Design Director, peers with Senior EMs and PMs
- Mandate: "evolve the Doctolib Design System to inherently support agentic AI components"
- Key outputs: trustworthy AI interfaces (EU AI Act compliant), agentic DS components, workflow handoffs between AI Phone → triage inbox → practitioner agenda
- Culture flag: "heavy-handed top-down management style, high pressure" — navigate by translating design to business KPIs

### CEO Quotes
- "Je revois le patient dans les yeux" (doctors look patients in the eyes again)
- "The caregiver remains in control of everything. It is they, in the end, who validate the summary, the referral letter, the prescription."
- Par la porte, ou par la fenêtre (Through the door, or through the window) — execution maxim
- Sovereign AI: Europe has talent, carbon-free energy, ethical frameworks to build without US/China dependency
